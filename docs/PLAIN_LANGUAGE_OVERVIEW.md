# Dream Team — In Plain Language

*A non-technical overview. If this captures what you have in your head, then we're aligned.*

---

## What you're building, in one sentence

**A whole digital agency that lives on your computer** — a team of 28 AI specialists that don't just give advice, they actually do the work — and a built-in supervisor that makes sure they never do anything they shouldn't.

Think of it like hiring a full company — a project manager, architects, developers, testers, marketers, analysts — except they all live inside an app, they work at machine speed, and they never need to be told the same thing twice.

---

## The problem it solves

Today's AI assistants are like a brilliant consultant who can only *talk*. You ask for a website, and they hand you instructions and code snippets — then **you** have to go build it.

There's a newer kind of AI (Manus.ai is the famous one) that actually *does* the work — it can use a computer, write the files, run the programs, browse the web. But it has no supervisor. It just acts. For a business, that's a liability: no record of what it did, no way to stop it from crossing a line, no proof it followed the rules.

**Your product is the missing combination: an AI team that does the work *and* a supervisor that governs and proves every move.** That supervisor is the part nobody else has, and it's the heart of the business.

---

## The final goal

You give it a project — typed, uploaded, or described in a chat — like:

> *"Build me a booking website for my clinic with a contact form, and put the bookings into a database."*

…and the team **plans it, builds it, tests it, and hands you a finished, working result** — while a supervisor checks every action against your rules and keeps a tamper-proof record of everything that happened. You watch it happen, and you stay in control: anything risky pauses and asks you first.

That's it. **Brief in → finished, governed work out.**

## A day in the life — the typical use case

Let's follow one real request, start to finish.

**1. You ask.**
You open the app and type: *"Build a booking website for my clinic. Clients pick a time, leave their name and email, and it saves to my database. Match my brand colors."* You hit go and pour a coffee.

**2. The project manager takes over.**
The **Orchestrator** (the head of the agency) reads your request, understands it's a building job, and assigns it to the right department — the **Build team**.

**3. The team does the work — for real.**
- The **Architect** decides how the site should be structured.
- The **Designer** lays out the booking page and applies your brand colors.
- The **Developer** writes the actual website files and wires the booking form to your database (Supabase).
- The **Tester** runs the site and confirms the form really submits and the booking really saves — not "looks done," but *actually works*.
- The **Truth-checker** verifies the team's claims are backed by real evidence before anything is called finished.

Each specialist hands off to the next with a clean summary — like a well-run office where nothing falls through the cracks.

**4. The supervisor watches every move.**
While they work, the **supervisor** sits between the team and your computer. Most of what they do is harmless — writing files inside a dedicated project folder — so it lets that flow at full speed. But the moment something *consequential* comes up — spending money, sending an email, touching anything outside the project folder, publishing live — it **pauses and asks you**: *"The team wants to do X. Allow it?"* You say yes or no. Nothing dangerous happens behind your back.

**5. Everything is recorded as proof.**
Every single action gets a sealed, tamper-proof receipt — *what was attempted, which rule applied, what was decided, and a digital signature*. If a regulator, a client, or you ever asks "show me exactly what this AI did," there's a complete, trustworthy answer. (This is the part that makes it sellable to serious businesses.)

**6. You get the result.**
A working booking website, the database wired up, tested, delivered — with a full record of how it was built and confirmation that no rules were crossed. Total time: minutes, not weeks.

## How it connects to the outside world

The agency isn't sealed off — it plugs into the tools a real business uses, and every connection is governed the same way (read freely, but ask before it writes or sends):

- **Its brain:** Claude (Anthropic's AI) — the intelligence behind every specialist.
- **Its memory & records:** Supabase — where all projects, history, and the proof-receipts are stored.
- **Calendars, drives, chat:** Google Calendar, Google Drive, Slack and more — the team can read your schedule or documents to do its job, and can post or send *only with your approval*.
- **The web:** it can browse to research and gather what it needs.

The rule is always the same: **reading is easy, acting on the outside world asks permission first.**
